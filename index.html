Evolution of Dystopian Fiction

How real events influence dystopian themes?

Have you imagined living in a perfect society - one without war, poverty, crime, disease, or conflict? If so, you're not alone.

Long ago, people were optimistic about the future and the promise of modern society.

However, building such societies is impossible.

Sir Thomas More gave the concept a name called "utopia" (greek for no place).

Attempts to build utopian societies has some compromises that oftentimes resulted to its complete opposite, dystopia (meaning bad place).

Though dystopia was coined a long time ago, it wasn't until the 20th century that the concept became more popular. Authors and filmmakers have used their works to tell stories about how people survive and endure life in dystopian societies.

Moreover, themes of their stores are oftentimes influenced by the events of a period.

Common dystopian themes

Common dystopian themes:

Oppressive Government

Usually set in a condition where people are under a totalitarian government, dictatorship, or fascism

Lack of Freedom

People are deprived of basic liberties. They lack freedom to choose and oftentimes forced to submit to a higher authority. Loss of individuality is also common here.

Ecological Issues

People here deal with the effects of environment degradation, climate change, and overpopulation. They also deal with lack of resources needed to survive.

Cyberpunk

Condition where people are dehumanized by machines and artificial intelligence. Also where robots have uncontrolled domination over humans

Bioligical Issues

Deals with bioligical problems like mass extinction, muttations, and reproductive issues.
