Evolution of Dystopian Fiction

How real events influence dystopian themes?

Have you imagined living in a perfect society - one without war, poverty, crime, disease, or conflict? If so, you're not alone.

Long ago, people were optimistic about the future and the promise of modern society.

However, building such societies is impossible.

Sir Thomas More gave the concept a name called "utopia" (greek for no place).

Attempts to build utopian societies has some compromises that oftentimes resulted to its complete opposite, dystopia (meaning bad place).
