Evolution of Dystopian Fiction

How real events influence dystopian themes?

Have you imagined living in a perfect society - one without war, poverty, crime, disease, or conflict? If so, you're not alone.

Long ago, people were optimistic about the future and the promise of modern society.

However, building such societies is impossible.

Sir Thomas More gave the concept a name called utopia (greek for no place).

Attempts to build utopian societies have some compromises that oftentimes resulted to their complete opposite, dystopia (meaning bad place).

Though dystopia was coined a long time ago, it wasn't until the 20th century that the concept became more popular. Authors and filmmakers have used their works to tell stories about how people survive and endure life in dystopian societies.

Moreover, themes of their stores are oftentimes influenced by the events of that period.

Common dystopian themes:

Oppressive Government

Usually set in a condition where people are under a totalitarian government, dictatorship, or fascism.

Lack of Freedom

People are deprived of basic liberties. They lack freedom to choose and oftentimes forced to submit to a higher authority. Loss of individuality is also common here.

Social Issues

Deals with the desctruction of society, normalization of violence, chaos, descrimination, inequality, and corruption. Caste system also exists here.

Ecological Issues

People here deal with the effects of environment degradation, climate change, and overpopulation. They also deal with lack of resources needed to survive.

Cyberpunk

Condition where people are dehumanized by machines and artificial intelligence; and where robots have uncontrolled domination over humans.

Biological Issues

Deals with biological problems like mass extinction, muttations, and reproductive issues.

About this project:

I have been a fan of dystopian fiction since the release of The Hunger Games back in 2012. Understanding dystopian fiction is a way to imagine how people deal with different circumstances that define them as a person.

This project is made to showcase how dystopian fiction has evolved throughout the decades since the beginning of the 20th century. Popular themes of dystopian fiction are influenced by real events or trends in a particular period.

Technology used: HTML, CSS, JQuery, AOS Library
